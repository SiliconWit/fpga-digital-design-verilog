Until now your Verilog has lived inside a simulator. An FPGA lets it become a real, running circuit. Inside the chip are thousands of small lookup tables and flip-flops that the toolchain configures to match your design. This lesson takes you through that flow end to end and finishes with your code blinking a real LED. #fpga #ice40 #verilog

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

1. **Describe** what is inside an FPGA: lookup tables, flip-flops, block RAM, and routing.
2. **Run** the open toolchain to synthesise, place, route, and flash a design.
3. **Map** top-level signals to physical pins with a constraints file.
4. **Read** a basic timing report and understand the maximum clock frequency.

:::tip[Instrument it]
Once a design runs on the board, the fastest way to see what it is really doing is to bring internal signals out to spare pins and watch them on a logic analyser. A low-cost USB analyser such as a Saleae clone, or a scope on a single pin, confirms your counter increments at the rate you expect and that the debounced button is clean. When a project grows past the bench, an [ESP32 or STM32](/education/sensor-actuator-interfacing-stm32/) can read a status line from the FPGA and [stream](/education/iot-systems/) uptime or error counts to a live dashboard, so a deployed board can [report](https://siliconwit.io) that it is still healthy.
:::

## Application Questions and Solutions

### Question 1: Works in simulation, wrong on the board

A counter behaves perfectly in simulation but on the FPGA it counts erratically when the button is pressed. What is the most likely cause?

<details>
<summary>**Click to reveal the solution**</summary>

<Steps>

1. **Suspect the input, not the logic.** Simulation used clean, ideal stimulus. A real mechanical button bounces, producing many fast edges per press. ✅

2. **Add a debouncer.** Sample the button on the clock and require it to be stable for a set number of cycles before accepting a change. ✅

3. **Confirm on hardware** with a logic analyser on the raw and debounced signals to see the bounce removed. ✅

</Steps>

</details>
